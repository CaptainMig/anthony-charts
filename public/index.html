How It Works
Anthony Charts is a living world intelligence dashboard ‚Äî not a news feed, not an opinion platform. Every number comes from a real, publicly available scientific or institutional source. Nothing is invented. Nothing is spin. The goal is to give you a clear, honest, sourced picture of the world across four dimensions: Earth, Space, Human Systems, and Biosphere.

Where we build composite indices (like World On Edge), we publish every weight and input here so you can evaluate our editorial choices yourself. Composite indices involve judgment ‚Äî we don't hide that.
The Philosophy
‚öñÔ∏è
Paired Opposites
Every destructive force has a countervailing force. Species extinction is paired with conservation coverage. Conflict deaths with peacekeeping reach. Wealth inequality with social mobility. This pairing is intentional ‚Äî it prevents the dashboard from being purely alarming, and it asks the more interesting question: is the response proportional to the problem?
üî¨
Trusted Sources Only
Every meter draws from peer-reviewed data, established institutional databases, or primary government science agencies. NOAA, NASA, IUCN, ACLED, UCDP, World Bank, UN DPPA, Copernicus C3S, NSIDC, Breakthrough Listen. No aggregators. No media outlets. No social signals. The source is always published in the drawer of each meter.
üìÖ
Update Cadence Matters
Each meter displays its update cadence (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, annual). We refresh values when the underlying source publishes. Some meters ‚Äî like Arctic Ice and Solar Activity ‚Äî update daily. Others, like Social Mobility and Conservation Coverage, update annually. Cadence is always shown on each card so you know what you're looking at.
üßÆ
Arc Scale Logic
Each arc gauge fills from 0% to 100% based on a meaningful scale ‚Äî not arbitrary. Temp Anomaly arc = position within the 0‚Äì2¬∞C Paris corridor. CO‚ÇÇ arc = position between 280ppm (pre-industrial) and 500ppm (extreme risk). Species Threat arc = threatened as % of all assessed. Scales are documented in each drawer and here below.

Calm Window Probability ‚Äî 28% (LOW)
This is not a prediction of peace. It is a probabilistic assessment of directional easing ‚Äî the likelihood that global tension meaningfully decreases within the next 12 months, based on current momentum in three signals:

¬∑ GPR 3-month slope (40% weight) ‚Äî Is the Geopolitical Risk Index trending up or down? A falling 3-month slope scores higher.
¬∑ ACLED conflict trend (40% weight) ‚Äî Are active conflict events growing or shrinking month-over-month? Declining event counts score higher.
¬∑ Volatility compression (20% weight) ‚Äî Is the GPR falling sharply enough to signal a genuine structural easing, not just noise? Sharp downward momentum scores higher.

Each input is scored 0‚Äì100 and weighted. The final Calm Window score reflects the probability of directional easing ‚Äî not cessation of conflict.
